Internet, AI evaluation, and large language model vocabulary practice.

Listen first. Then repeat after each expression and sentence.

1. Result.
A result is the direct output of an evaluation, such as a score, success rate, or failure rate.
Readers should distinguish the result from the claim it is used to support.

2. Interpretation.
Interpretation explains what an evaluation result means and how far the evidence can reasonably go.
Careful interpretation prevents readers from overextending a narrow benchmark result.

3. Assessment.
Assessment means a broader judgment based on tests, evidence, review, and analysis.
A safety assessment may combine evaluations, expert review, and deployment evidence.

4. Specify.
Specify means to state something clearly and precisely.
Evaluation reports should specify the tested system, budget, and validity checks.

5. Explicitly.
Explicitly means directly and clearly stated, rather than implied.
Useful reports explicitly describe the claim being tested and the evidence behind the result.

6. Account for.
Account for means to include a factor in the analysis or adjust for its effect.
Evaluators should account for contamination before interpreting high scores.

7. Distort results.
Distort results means to make results misleading, either higher or lower than the true signal.
Broken problems, refusals, and reward hacking can distort results.

8. Inflate scores.
Inflate scores means to make scores appear higher than they should be.
Contamination can inflate scores by rewarding memorization instead of generalization.

9. Understate risk.
Understate risk means to make a risk appear smaller or less serious than it really is.
Weak safeguard testing can understate risk by ignoring realistic attacker resources.

10. Reasoning traces.
Reasoning traces are intermediate records of a model's thinking or planning process.
Reasoning traces can help evaluators detect evaluation awareness or sandbagging.

11. Specify which claim.
Specify which claim means to clearly identify the exact conclusion an evaluation is designed to test.
The evaluation setup should specify which claim it is designed to test.

12. Reveal model capabilities.
Reveal model capabilities means to show what a model can do under appropriate elicitation.
Strong elicitation can reveal model capabilities that a weak prompt would miss.

13. Validity checks for contamination and reward hacking.
Validity checks for contamination and reward hacking examine whether scores reflect genuine performance.
A good evaluation report should include validity checks for contamination and reward hacking.

14. Measured success rate.
Measured success rate is the observed percentage of tasks completed successfully under a specific setup.
Changing the harness may materially affect the measured success rate.

15. Deliver real-world utility.
Deliver real-world utility means to produce practical value in real scenarios, not only high benchmark scores.
The model delivers real-world utility by completing long-horizon coding tasks reliably.
