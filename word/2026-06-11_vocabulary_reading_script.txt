Vocabulary and expressions.

1. validly support. The evidence may support the claim, but only within the limits of the evaluation setup. A trustworthy report explains what the results validly support.

2. measured capability ceiling. A score under one setup is not always a measured capability ceiling. Better elicitation may reveal stronger performance.

3. safeguard testing. Safeguard testing checks whether safety controls remain effective under realistic attacks. Weak testing can understate risk.

4. account for. The analysis should account for the resources available to realistic attackers. If it does not account for the harness, the conclusion may be misleading.

5. realistic attackers. A relevant threat model describes realistic attackers, not unlimited attackers. Realistic attackers may still have tools, time, and custom workflows.

6. validity checks. Evaluation reports should pair headline scores with validity checks. Validity checks help readers judge whether the score reflects intended behavior.

7. distorted by known hazards. A benchmark result can be distorted by known hazards such as contamination or reward hacking. The report should explain how those hazards were handled.

8. unsupported claims. A model that makes fewer unsupported claims is more useful in professional workflows. Unsupported claims should be surfaced and corrected early.

9. surface uncertainty. Good analysis should surface uncertainty rather than hide it. Users need to know when the evidence is incomplete.

10. background process. A background process can curate memory from chat history. The process should avoid stale, incorrect, or irrelevant information.

Review expressions.

11. forward-looking statements. Forward-looking statements describe expectations about future events. They are not guarantees of future performance.

12. speak only as of the date hereof. These statements speak only as of the date hereof. The phrase limits the statement to the current document date.

13. except as required by law. Except as required by law, the company disclaims any obligation to update the statement. This phrase creates a legal exception.

14. disclaim any obligation to update. The company may disclaim any obligation to update forward-looking statements. This means it says it has no duty to revise them later.

15. on a user's behalf. Information agents can monitor information on a user's behalf. They can send updates and links that help the user dive deeper and take action.
