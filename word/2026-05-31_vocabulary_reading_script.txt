Internet, AI product writing, and large language model vocabulary practice.

Listen first. Then repeat after each expression and sentence.

1. Reason.
Reason means to analyze information through logical steps before reaching a conclusion.
Advanced models can reason through complex tasks instead of only matching patterns.

2. Infer.
Infer means to reach a conclusion from limited evidence or indirect clues.
The system can infer user intent from previous messages and task context.

3. Detect.
Detect means to identify whether an object, signal, or pattern is present.
The classifier can detect unsafe content before the response is shown to users.

4. Deliver.
Deliver means to produce, achieve, or bring a measurable result.
The new model delivers stronger coding performance on long-horizon tasks.

5. Coupled with.
Coupled with means used together with another component to create a stronger overall system.
Coupled with a retrieval layer, the model can answer domain-specific questions more reliably.

6. Integrated with.
Integrated with means deeply built into another system or workflow.
The assistant is integrated with the company's internal search and documentation tools.

7. Demanding.
Demanding means requiring high performance, reliability, resources, or effort.
Enterprise AI systems must handle demanding workloads with stable latency.

8. On the fly.
On the fly means generated or changed immediately during runtime.
The application builds custom summaries on the fly based on each user's query.

9. Claim.
A claim is a statement, hypothesis, or conclusion that an evaluation is designed to test.
The evaluation setup should clearly specify which claim it is testing.

10. Valid evidence.
Valid evidence is evidence that meaningfully supports a conclusion or evaluation result.
Useful reports explain why the available evidence is valid for the claim being tested.

11. Regression.
A regression is a new failure or performance drop introduced by a recent change.
The team ran evaluation tests to detect regressions before releasing the model.

12. Mitigation.
Mitigation refers to actions that reduce risk, harm, or failure impact.
The paper proposes several mitigation strategies for hallucination in generated answers.

13. Compliance.
Compliance means following legal, security, privacy, or organizational requirements.
AI systems in regulated industries must support compliance audits.

14. Redaction.
Redaction removes or masks sensitive information from text or documents.
The pipeline applies redaction before sending user data to the model.

15. Deduplication.
Deduplication removes duplicate records, documents, or examples from a dataset.
Dataset deduplication can reduce memorization and improve training efficiency.
